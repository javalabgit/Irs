## 2-Mark Questions

### Module 1

1. **What is meant by problem identification in entrepreneurship?**  
   Problem identification is the process of recognizing and understanding real-world issues or gaps that can be converted into potential business opportunities.

2. **What is the purpose of developing a macro industry–problem perspective?**  
   It helps entrepreneurs view problems from a broader industry level, identify emerging trends, and discover large-scale opportunities for innovation.

3. **What does assembling ‘real-world’ problems involve?**  
   It involves collecting, listing, and observing practical issues faced by people, industries, or communities that can inspire innovative solutions.

4. **Why is it important to examine chosen problems before developing solutions?**  
   Examining problems ensures a deep understanding of the root causes and helps in determining whether the problem is significant, solvable, and worth pursuing.

5. **What does problem analysis help an entrepreneur achieve?**  
   Problem analysis helps break down complex issues, understand their causes and effects, and identify the best opportunities for creating impactful solutions.

### Module 2

1. **What is the main goal of customer identification?**  
   The goal of customer identification is to recognize and define specific customer segments that share similar needs, behaviors, or characteristics.

2. **What is meant by ‘Jobs-To-Be-Done’ (JTBD) in customer analysis?**  
   JTBD refers to understanding what task or goal customers are trying to accomplish with a product or service, focusing on their desired outcomes rather than just product features.

3. **What is a customer persona?**  
   A customer persona is a detailed, semi-fictional profile of an ideal customer created using data and insights to represent their goals, motivations, and challenges.

4. **What is the importance of customer validation?**  
   Customer validation confirms that the identified problem and proposed solution genuinely meet the target customers’ needs and expectations.

5. **What is the purpose of pivoting or refining the customer-problem fit?**  
   Pivoting or refining helps entrepreneurs adjust their approach or solution based on feedback to ensure the idea is feasible and aligned with customer needs.

### Module 3

1. **What is the main purpose of ideation in entrepreneurship?**  
   Ideation aims to generate creative and innovative ideas that can solve identified customer problems effectively.

2. **What are ideation techniques?**  
   Ideation techniques are structured methods such as brainstorming, mind mapping, or SCAMPER used to generate multiple creative solutions.

3. **Why is it important to examine different ideation techniques?**  
   Examining various ideation techniques helps entrepreneurs choose the most effective approach for generating diverse and practical ideas.

4. **What does solution idea generation involve?**  
   Solution idea generation involves developing multiple possible solutions for a defined problem using creative thinking and innovation tools.

5. **How does ideation contribute to entrepreneurial success?**  
   Ideation encourages innovation, helps identify unique value propositions, and increases the chances of developing a successful business solution.

### Module 4

1. **What is meant by opportunity mapping in entrepreneurship?**  
   Opportunity mapping is the process of identifying potential business opportunities by analyzing market gaps, trends, and customer needs.

2. **Why is it important to identify global competitors?**  
   Identifying global competitors helps entrepreneurs understand market standards, benchmark best practices, and find ways to differentiate their products or services.

3. **What does TAM stand for, and what does it represent?**  
   TAM stands for Total Addressable Market, which represents the total revenue opportunity available if a product or service achieved 100% market share.

4. **How does reviewing industry trends help in business planning?**  
   Reviewing industry trends helps predict future market demands, adapt to changes, and align business strategies with growth opportunities.

5. **What is the purpose of financial planning in entrepreneurship?**  
   Financial planning helps entrepreneurs estimate costs, forecast revenue, and ensure the venture’s financial sustainability and profitability.

### Module 5

1. **What is a prototype in entrepreneurship?**  
   A prototype is an early sample or model of a product created to test and demonstrate its key features and functionality.

2. **What does MVP stand for, and what does it mean?**  
   MVP stands for Minimum Viable Product — it is the simplest version of a product that allows testing of core features with early users to gather feedback.

3. **What is the main purpose of building an initial prototype?**  
   The main purpose is to visualize the idea, identify design or usability issues, and test whether the concept works in practice.

4. **Why are prototypes important for early validation?**  
   Prototypes help validate the idea by allowing real users or stakeholders to interact with it and provide feedback before full-scale development.

5. **How does an MVP help entrepreneurs reduce risk?**  
   An MVP helps entrepreneurs test their assumptions with minimal resources, identify customer needs early, and avoid costly mistakes.

### Module 6

1. **What is meant by feasibility assessment in entrepreneurship?**  
   Feasibility assessment is the process of evaluating whether a business idea is practical, profitable, and sustainable in the current market environment.

2. **Why is it important to map your relative position in the market?**  
   Mapping your market position helps identify how your product or service compares to competitors in terms of value, price, and customer perception.

3. **What does estimating opportunity size involve?**  
   Estimating opportunity size involves calculating the potential market demand, revenue, and growth scope for a business idea.

4. **How does opportunity assessment support decision-making?**  
   It provides data-driven insights that help entrepreneurs decide whether to proceed, modify, or drop a business idea based on feasibility.

5. **What key factors determine market feasibility?**  
   Key factors include market demand, competition, customer willingness to pay, resource availability, and potential profitability.

### Module 7

1. **What is a business model?**  
   A business model explains how a company creates, delivers, and captures value through its products, services, and operations.

2. **What is a revenue model?**  
   A revenue model defines how a business earns income from its products or services — for example, through sales, subscriptions, or advertising.

3. **Why is it important to examine different revenue models?**  
   Examining various revenue models helps entrepreneurs choose the most sustainable and profitable way to generate income for their startup.

4. **What is the Lean Canvas model?**  
   The Lean Canvas is a one-page business planning tool that outlines key elements such as the problem, customer segments, value proposition, and revenue streams.

5. **What is the purpose of building a Lean Canvas for a startup?**  
   Building a Lean Canvas helps organize and visualize business ideas clearly, enabling entrepreneurs to validate assumptions and refine their strategies effectively.

### Module 8

1. **What is the main purpose of marketing in entrepreneurship?**  
   Marketing aims to create awareness, attract potential customers, and communicate the value of a product or service effectively.

2. **How does sales differ from marketing?**  
   Marketing focuses on attracting and engaging customers, while sales focuses on converting those customers into actual buyers.

3. **What does GTM stand for in business strategy?**  
   GTM stands for Go-To-Market, which refers to the plan a company uses to deliver its product to customers and achieve competitive advantage.

4. **Why is a Go-To-Market (GTM) strategy important?**  
   A GTM strategy ensures that the product reaches the right audience through the right channels at the right time, maximizing market success.

5. **What is meant by mapping a sales process?**  
   Mapping a sales process involves outlining each step from customer contact to deal closure to ensure consistency, efficiency, and measurable results.

### Module 9

1. **What is financial planning in entrepreneurship?**  
   Financial planning involves forecasting revenues, estimating expenses, and allocating resources to ensure business sustainability and growth.

2. **Why is profitability analysis important?**  
   It helps entrepreneurs understand whether their business can generate more revenue than expenses and remain financially viable.

3. **What are the key components of financial planning?**  
   The main components include budgeting, cash flow management, cost estimation, and revenue forecasting.

4. **What is meant by bootstrapping?**  
   Bootstrapping is building a business using personal savings and internal cash flow without relying on external funding.

5. **Name two common funding options for startups.**  
   Angel investment and venture capital funding.

### Module 10

1. **Why is finding the right co-founder important in a startup?**  
   A co-founder brings complementary skills, shares responsibilities, and helps make strategic decisions for the startup’s success.

2. **What role do mentors play in entrepreneurship?**  
   Mentors provide guidance, industry insights, support decision-making, and help avoid common mistakes in the startup journey.

3. **What is the main goal of building an initial team?**  
   The goal is to assemble a group of skilled individuals who can execute the startup idea effectively and drive early growth.

4. **What factors should be considered when selecting team members?**  
   Skills, experience, cultural fit, commitment, and the ability to work collaboratively toward shared goals.

5. **How can a strong team impact a startup’s success?**  
   A strong team improves execution, innovation, problem-solving, and increases the likelihood of achieving business objectives.

## 10-Mark Questions

### Module 1

#### Q1: Build a Problem Statement Canvas for Hostel Late-Night Snacks

**Core Problem:**  
Hostel residents do not have access to affordable and healthy snacks late at night. Existing food delivery apps are either expensive or unavailable after midnight.

**Target Users:**  
College hostel students, especially those staying up late to study or engaged in night activities.

**Importance:**  
Poor access to healthy snacks leads to unhealthy eating habits, reduced productivity, and inconvenience. Addressing this gap enhances student health and overall satisfaction.

**Existing Alternatives:**  
- Food delivery apps (high cost, limited timing)  
- Local shops (close early)  
- Instant noodles and processed snacks (unhealthy)

**Success Criteria:**  
Available 24x7, healthy snacks priced affordably (<₹50/item), minimum variety of 8–10 options, convenience (located within hostel/campus), and at least 70% student satisfaction/adoption during pilot.

### Module 2

#### Q1: Customer Segmentation and Business Validation for Healthy Snack Subscription

**Customer Segmentation:**  
- Primary: Students consuming snacks regularly  
- Secondary: Parents funding snacks  
- Psychographic: Health-conscious students

**Validation:**  
Conduct surveys and taste trials to check willingness to pay for healthier alternatives; use feedback to refine the offering.

#### Q2: Creating Customer Persona and Validating Need for Study-Hours Tracking App

**Customer Persona:**  
Example – a 20-year-old tech-savvy student struggling with time management and routinely uses mobile apps.

**Validation:**  
Pilot the app with similar students, collect usage data and feedback, and refine features accordingly.

#### Q3: Validating Transport Problem for Bike-Sharing Startup

Conduct interviews at multiple campus locations, survey willingness to pay, use mock sign-ups. If over 60% express need and interest, the problem and solution are validated.

Use brainstorming and SCAMPER techniques to generate ideas like AI flashcards, gamified quizzes, and collaborative study groups. Evaluate on feasibility, cost, and potential engagement.

#### Q2: Using Solution Idea Generation to Reduce Plastic Bottles

### Module 4

#### Q1: Competitor Analysis and Opportunity Identification for Online Tutoring

Analyze local and global competitors, pricing, features, and adoption; identify gaps such as personalized learning and low-cost mobile solutions. Develop an affordable AI-driven personalized tutoring platform as an opportunity.

#### Q2: Estimating TAM for College Skill Development Platform

Calculate total student population, digital adoption rates, and willingness to pay to estimate TAM, then SAM and SOM based on reachable market segments.

#### Q3: Competitive Landscape Mapping for Electric Scooters

Identify direct and indirect competitors, benchmark pricing and service, analyze innovation trends, and position your startup uniquely based on gaps.

### Module 5

#### Q1: Prototyping for Campus Meal-Sharing App

A prototype is essential to test assumptions and gather user feedback before full development. Use low-fidelity paper sketches or wireframes and high-fidelity interactive mockups. Validate usability, affordability, and trust with target users.

#### Q2: MVP Development for Peer-to-Peer Tutoring

Create a minimal product with student registration, tutor matching, and session scheduling. Pilot with a small group to validate engagement and feasibility.

---

### Module 6

#### Q1: Market Feasibility Assessment for Personalized Learning App

Map competitors and alternatives. Estimate opportunity size using student population, adoption rates, and average spend. Assess projected revenue versus costs to conclude viability.

#### Q2: Calculating TAM, SAM, and SOM for Biodegradable Packaging Startup

Use top-down industry data and bottom-up local surveys to estimate the total market (TAM), serviceable portions (SAM), and realistically obtainable market share (SOM).

---

### Module 7

#### Q1: Selecting Revenue Model for Hospital Laundry Service App

Subscription (weekly/monthly) is ideal for predictable recurring revenue. Include delivery charges to boost sustainability. Analyze unit economics to ensure profitability.

#### Q2: Building a Lean Canvas for a Startup Idea

Outline each section of Lean Canvas – problem, customer segments, unique value proposition, solution, channels, revenue streams, cost structure, key metrics, and unfair advantage. Validate assumptions with customer interviews, market tests, and pilot launches.

---

### Module 8

#### Q1: Go-To-Market Strategy for Skill Development App

Target students through social media, campus ambassadors, and workshops. Use free trials and referral programs to build traction. Monitor conversion and engagement rates to optimize marketing.

#### Q2: Sales Process Mapping for Healthy Snack Delivery

Steps include awareness (posters, social media), interest (sample tasting), consideration (subscription plans), purchase (easy online ordering), and retention (loyalty rewards). Track metrics for continuous improvement.

---

### Module 9

#### Q1: Financial Plan for Peer-to-Peer Tutoring

Estimate development, marketing, operational costs. Forecast revenue from subscriptions and commissions. Build cash flow plan to ensure sustainability and preparedness for funding needs.

#### Q2: Bootstrapping vs Angel Funding for Startup

Bootstrapping uses personal resources, maintains control but limits growth. Angel funding accelerates development, provides mentorship, but dilutes ownership.
